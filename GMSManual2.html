Game Management System Guide

The intended audience for this guide is lottery users who only access the Game Management System’s (GMS) Retailer and Owner modules. GMS is the master control center for administrating and managing all draw games, instant games, accounting, reports, terminals, retailers, and store owners.

    Documentation Process

    After determining that this is the type of document needed for a project, I use the following process to complete the initial draft:

    1. If necessary, meet with the project manager to obtain the names and contact information of each team member (software engineers, SMEs, product manager, trainers, lottery staff, etc.)

    2. Compile all software-related documents (system requirements, functional specs, addendums, technical manuals, etc.) from our department’s repository. These documents were usually written by me or another senior technical writer on the team.

    3. Work together with the product manager, field service team, software engineers, and software architect to obtain access to the terminal and acquire any additional information I may need about it.

    4. Once I get access to the terminal, I start using the terminal and writing down any questions I have about it.

    5. If necessary, request a 30-minute to an hour meeting with the software engineers and field service team to address any questions or concerns I have.

    6. Create an outline based on the information I acquired.

    7. If necessary, go over the outline with the lottery, SG’s training team, and other important stakeholders.

    8. Once we reach a consensus, I would write the initial draft and continue following our department’s documentation process.
